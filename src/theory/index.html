AND-OR-NOT NORs

exclusive or inclusive or (disjunction)

  1. For each of the following sentences, determine whether the intended meaning is exclusive or, or the intended meaning is inclusive or (disjunction), in common speech.
    1. Experience with React (R) or Angular (A) is required (Q)
      A: inclusive OR could be both or either - Q = R||A
    2. A password(P) must have a special character (S) or contain at least eight characters (P.length)
      A: inclusive OR could be both or either - P = P[S] || (P.length>=8)
    3. Our dinner (D) package includes three courses (C) and wine (W) or two courses and champagne (H)
      A: exclusive OR only one meal possible - D = (3C && W) || (2C && H)
    4. School (S) is closed if it's under -20 (C) or seven feet (F) of snow fall overnight (O)
      A: inclusive OR could be both or either - S = C || F
    5. For 50000 miles (M), you can get two seats in economy (E) or one seat in business (B) class
      A: exclusive OR only one seating possible - M = 2E || B
  2. State the converse (B>A), inverse (!A>!B) and contrapositive (!B>!A) of the following implications:
    1. If it doesn't snow tonight, I'll go to a movie.
      • Co: If I go to a movie tonight it won't snow.
      • In: If it snows tonight I won't go to a movie.
      • Cp: I won't go to a movie if it snows tonight.
    2. If my car is outside and it snows, I'll need to brush snow off in the morning.
      • Co: If I brush snow off in the morning, my car is outside and it'll snow.
      • In: If my car isn't outside and it snows, I won't need to brush the snow off in the morning.
      • Cp: I won't need to brush the snow off in the morning if my car isn't outside and it snows.
    3. If you excel in JavaScript, you will find a great job.
      • Co: If you find a great job, you'll excel in JavaScript.
      • In: If you don't excel in JavaScript, you won't find a great job.
      • Cp: If you don't find a great job, you won't excel in JavaScript.
    4. A positive integer is a prime only if it has no divisors other than 1 and itself.
      • Co: If a prime has no divisors other than 1 and itself, it is a positive integer.
      • In: A positive integer isn't a prime if it has more divisors than 1 and itself.
      • Cp: If a prime has divisors other than 1 and itself, it isn't a positive integer.
  3. Can you emulate AND statement using only NOT and OR statements? Imagine that you have only NOT (negation) and OR (disjunction) at your disposal. You have propositions p1 and p2, and you need to emulate their conjunction (p1 AND p2) using only negation and disjunction. How can you do that?
    • P1 && P2 = !(!P1||!P2)

Maverick's hawk

When Maverick sees the hawk, he says to Annabelle, "You know what that hawk means? Absolutely nothing." This is a sly reference to Ladyhawke (1985) which was also directed by Richard Donner and produced by his then future-wife, Lauren Shuler.
